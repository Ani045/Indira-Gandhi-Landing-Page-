Common Eye Conditions

Recognizing eye conditions early is crucial for preserving your vision. Here are some of the most common eye issues treated at IGEHRC :

Cataract

Clouding of the natural lens in the eye that typically occurs with age. We offer advanced cataract surgery using the latest technology to restore clear vision.

Glaucoma

A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often linked to high eye pressure. Early detection and management are key to preventing vision loss.

Corneal Conditions

Various issues related to the cornea, including keratoconus and infections, requiring specialized treatments and surgeries like corneal transplants.

Refractive Errors

Vision problems such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism that can be corrected with LASIK surgery or other refractive treatments.

Macular Degeneration

Deterioration of the central portion of the retina, leading to vision loss in the central visual field. Early intervention is essential to managing this condition.

Retinal Diseases

Conditions affecting the retina, such as diabetic retinopathy or retinal detachment. We offer comprehensive treatment for these potentially sight-threatening conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our eye care services, treatments, and procedures.

If you experience blurry vision, frequent headaches, eye strain, or difficulty seeing at night, it's time for an eye check-up. Even without symptoms, regular screenings are important for maintaining good eye health.
Cataracts can cause blurry or cloudy vision, sensitivity to light, difficulty seeing at night, and seeing halos around lights. If you notice these symptoms, consulting an eye specialist is recommended.
LASIK is a painless procedure since numbing eye drops are used. Some patients may feel slight pressure, but discomfort is minimal. Recovery is usually quick, and most people notice improved vision within a day.
Glaucoma is an eye condition that damages the optic nerve, often due to high eye pressure. It has no early symptoms and can lead to blindness if untreated. While there is no cure, early detection and treatment can slow its progression.
Premature babies, especially those born before 32 weeks or weighing less than 1500g, are at risk for Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP). A screening within the first month is essential to detect and treat the condition early.
Yes, diabetes can cause diabetic retinopathy, which damages the blood vessels in the retina and may lead to vision loss. Regular eye exams are crucial for early detection and management.
