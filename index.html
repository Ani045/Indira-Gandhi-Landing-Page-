Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our eye care services, treatments, and procedures.

Adults should have a comprehensive eye exam every 1-2 years, depending on age, risk factors, and physical condition. Children should have their first eye exam at 6 months of age, again at age 3, and before first grade.
Common signs include blurred vision, eye strain, frequent headaches, seeing spots or floaters, difficulty with night vision, and changes in vision. If you experience any of these symptoms, you should schedule an eye examination.
We accept most major insurance plans including VSP, EyeMed, and many others. Please contact our office to verify if we accept your specific insurance plan and learn about our flexible payment options.
The LASIK procedure itself typically takes about 15-20 minutes for both eyes. However, you should plan to be at the surgery center for 2-3 hours for preparation and initial recovery.
Most patients see better within 1-3 days after surgery. Complete recovery typically takes about 4-6 weeks. We provide detailed post-operative care instructions to ensure optimal healing and results.
Some eye conditions can be hereditary. If you have a family history of eye problems, we recommend regular comprehensive eye exams for your children to ensure early detection and treatment of any potential issues.
